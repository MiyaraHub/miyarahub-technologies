How It Works

Direct Network Connection. No Cloud Required.

AVR Maestro communicates directly with your receiver over Wi-Fi using the same network protocols your receiver already supports. Your data never touches an external server.

1

Add Your Receiver

Enter the IP address of your Denon or Marantz receiver. AVR Maestro instantly tests HTTP, Telnet, and HEOS connectivity and detects available features.

2

Connect Over Wi-Fi

AVR Maestro communicates via HTTP for reliable status polling and Telnet for real-time event streaming. HEOS provides now-playing info with album art.

3

Take Full Control

Adjust volume, switch inputs, select surround modes, manage zones, fine-tune Audyssey/Dirac/IMAX settings, and control your entire system from one app.

Security & Privacy

Local Only. No Account. No Cloud.

AVR Maestro is designed with a privacy-first, local-only architecture. No cloud dependency, no user account, no data collection beyond crash reporting.

🔒

No Cloud Relay

All commands and status polling happen directly between your phone and your receiver over your local Wi-Fi. Nothing leaves your network.

👤

No Account Required

No sign-up, no email, no MiyaraHub account needed. Open the app, enter your receiver's IP, and you're in control.

📡

Minimal Analytics

Firebase Crashlytics for bug reports only. No personal data, no usage tracking, no ads. Your receiver data never leaves your phone.

💾

Local Storage Only

Device IP addresses and app preferences are stored locally on your device using SharedPreferences. Nothing is synced to a server.

🛡️

User-Initiated Actions Only

The app sends commands to your receiver only on your explicit action. Nothing happens automatically or in the background without your knowledge.

📶

Network Permission Only

The only device permission AVR Maestro requires is internet/network access, used exclusively to connect to your receiver on your local Wi-Fi.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about AVR Maestro.

Which receivers are supported? ▼
AVR Maestro works with networked Denon and Marantz AV receivers that support IP control (HTTP and/or Telnet) - most models from 2014 onward. The app auto-detects available features on connection. Reachability comes down to your network, not just the model: if the receiver's own web portal, the HEOS app, and the official Denon or Marantz app can all reach it on the same network - and its network ports aren't blocked - AVR Maestro should see it too.
How does AVR Maestro communicate with my receiver? ▼
AVR Maestro uses three protocols: HTTP for reliable status polling and commands, Telnet for real-time event streaming (volume changes, input switches), and HEOS for now-playing information with album art. All communication happens directly on your local Wi-Fi network.
Does it support Audyssey, Dirac Live, and IMAX Enhanced? ▼
Yes. AVR Maestro provides full control over Audyssey MultEQ, Dynamic EQ, Dynamic Volume, LFC, and Containment. It also supports Dirac Live filter slot switching, IMAX Enhanced mode with crossover settings, and Auro-3D configuration. Available features depend on what your specific receiver model supports.
Can I control multiple zones? ▼
Yes. AVR Maestro supports Zone 2 and Zone 3 with independent power, volume, input source, and quick presets. Each zone can be controlled from a dedicated section, and zone status is visible at a glance from the main dashboard.
What are Scenes? ▼
A Scene is a one-tap snapshot of your whole setup - input, volume, sound mode, Audyssey/Dirac selection, speaker preset, channel levels, zones, and even the tuner. Save as many as you like (movie night, gaming, late-night listening) and recall any of them instantly. It's the fastest way to jump between the ways you actually use your system.
How much does AVR Maestro cost? ▼
AVR Maestro is a one-time purchase at $9.99. No subscription, no ads, no in-app purchases. You buy it once and own it.
Can I control my receiver remotely from outside my home? ▼
AVR Maestro connects to whatever IP address you configure. If your receiver is accessible via VPN or Tailscale, the app will work remotely. We recommend using a VPN or Tailscale rather than exposing your receiver directly to the internet.
Which platforms and versions are supported? ▼
AVR Maestro is available on iPhone (App Store), Android (Google Play), Windows (Microsoft Store) and macOS (Mac App Store). On Android it requires Android 5.0 (API 21) or higher; on iPhone, a recent device on a current iOS release.
